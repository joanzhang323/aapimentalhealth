The Model Minority Myth

Model Minority: a stereotype that inaccurately portrays Asian American & Pacific Islanders (AAPIs) as successfully integrating into mainstream culture and having overcome the challenges of racial bias

Problem: The Model Minority Myth neglects the diversity within the AAPI community and fails to account for ethnic, gender, socioeconomic, and educational differences. Consequently, this myth masks the reality of mental illness prevalence in the AAPI community and may worsen mental health by...

01.

Increasing Pressures

AAPI's feel increased pressure to meet high academic achievements and perfection that are associated with the Model Minority stereotype. This stereotype also drives standards to be set higher for Asian American students and pushes them to fit this extremely high, yet narrow standard.

02.

Heightening Stigma

People within the AAPI community internalize the stereotype and start to believe that mental illness is a problem of weakness that sets them apart from a high-achieving community. This enhances the silence and stigma associated with mental illness and prevents productive dialogue and treatment-seeking.

03.

Cutting Funds

Funds to support mental health research are cut, which starts a perpetual cycle that prevents accurate information on minority mental health to be recorded and shared. Furthermore, less funding goes to adapting mental health treatment to be more culturally sensitive due to a perceived "lack of need."

Behind the Data

  • Through exploring the first visualization, we hope you were able to explore the data and make note of specific demographics within the AAPI community that have major mental health concerns.

  • However, we wanted to personalize our data to put real faces to each Asian American surveyed in this study.

  • With the help of our friends, we were able to represent the anonymized individuals in our final visualization through the faces of students at Harvard and various other universities.

  • Our goal was to humanize the data and to show that mental illnesses are more than numbers or statistics.

  • Mental illnesses aren’t just dots or squares on a page, but the real experiences and struggles of human beings.

  • Mental illnesses do not discriminate. It could happen to anyone- your brother, sister, parent, friend, roommate, and possibly even you.

  • We encourage you to fight the stigma against mental health in the AAPI community…

  • By starting conversations with those around you about their mental health experiences…

  • By going beyond a superficial “how are you doing?” when you sense a friend is struggling…

  • By prioritizing your mental health through self-care, exercise, nutrition, and sufficient sleep...

  • By seeking counseling even when you think the problem will just past…

  • By challenging the idea that mental health is weakness and that you’re “strong enough.”

  • In our final visualization, you will be able to see how various treatment interventions can play a role in saving lives at different stages of the suicide process.

  • While the faces represented are not of individuals who have necessarily thought, planned, or attempted suicide, they represent numerous Asian American college students who have struggled with suicide.

  • We hope that this final visualization allows you to be a resource to your friends and/or encourages you to seek the help you may need

  • Each dot and rectangle on this page is a person, life, and story that deserves to be heard.

  • Help us preserve and build up each individual in our community by prioritizing AAPI Mental Health.

  • Take care!
